# Reflections & Lessons Learned

## Skills Gained Throughout the Course

Throughout this course, I gained hands-on experience with several cybersecurity tools, platforms, and concepts that strengthened both my technical knowledge and problem-solving abilities. Working with tools such as Splunk, Sysmon, Wireshark, Microsoft Azure, Metasploit, and Cisco Packet Tracer helped me better understand how cybersecurity professionals monitor systems, analyze logs, investigate suspicious activity, and secure enterprise environments.

The projects and labs also improved my understanding of networking, endpoint security, SIEM monitoring, Active Directory, Group Policy configuration, and system hardening. Completing practical exercises allowed me to apply classroom concepts in realistic cybersecurity scenarios instead of only learning theory.

---

## Challenges Encountered & How I Addressed Them

One challenge I encountered during this course was troubleshooting technical issues within lab environments. At times, configuring tools such as Splunk, Sysmon, Metasploit, or Active Directory policies required multiple attempts before working correctly. Learning how to diagnose errors, review logs, and carefully follow technical procedures helped me become more patient and detail-oriented during troubleshooting.

Another challenge was learning how different cybersecurity tools connect together within larger systems. Initially, understanding SIEM workflows, event logging, and security monitoring processes felt overwhelming. However, through repeated practice and hands-on labs, I became more comfortable analyzing logs, identifying security events, and understanding how monitoring tools support Security Operations Center (SOC) environments.

---

## How My Understanding of Cybersecurity Has Developed

Before taking this course, I primarily viewed cybersecurity as protecting systems from hackers and malware. Through these projects and labs, I developed a much broader understanding of cybersecurity operations, including system monitoring, policy enforcement, network defense, security analysis, and incident response concepts.

The hands-on experience demonstrated how important visibility and monitoring are in cybersecurity. Projects involving Sysmon, Splunk, and Microsoft Sentinel showed how analysts use logs and security events to investigate suspicious behavior and identify potential threats. Additionally, labs involving Active Directory and Group Policy helped me understand how organizations enforce security standards within enterprise environments.

Working on the VICEROY Symposium research project also expanded my understanding of how cybersecurity intersects with the electromagnetic spectrum (EMS), communications systems, and national security operations.

---

## Areas I Am Continuing to Improve

As I continue developing my cybersecurity skills, I want to improve my knowledge in cloud security, threat hunting, digital forensics, and enterprise-level security operations. I also plan to continue building hands-on experience through certifications, cybersecurity competitions, and independent lab projects.

In the future, I hope to continue developing the technical and analytical skills necessary for roles in security operations, incident response, and cybersecurity analysis.
